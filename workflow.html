Step 01 · 10 minutes
Set Up Your AI Workspace
⏱ 10 minutes

Before you can use AI effectively, you need to set it up to sound like you — not like the average of the internet.

1
Open ChatGPT.com
Sign up for free. Select GPT-4o (available on the free plan). Open a new conversation.
2
Set Custom Instructions
Go to Settings > Custom Instructions. Describe your business: what you do, who you serve, your tone, what you never say. In the second box: 'Give me options, use short sentences, write in first person.'
3
Open Claude.ai in a second tab
Claude is better for nuance and brand voice. ChatGPT is faster for ideation. Having both open lets you choose the right tool for each task.
4
Run your first test prompt
Paste: 'Write 3 Instagram captions about [your topic] for [your audience]. Tone: [3 adjectives]. Max 120 characters each. End each with a question.' If it does not sound like you — update your Custom Instructions and retry.
✓ Step Deliverable
Your AI workspace set up and personalised to your brand voice.
Step 02 · 20 minutes
Create a Full Week of Content
⏱ 20 minutes

One AI session. One pillar piece. Everything else generated from it automatically.

1
Generate the pillar piece
Paste: 'I run [business] targeting [audience]. Write a 400-word LinkedIn article about [your topic this week]. Structure: hook that challenges a common assumption, 3 key insights, a CTA asking readers to follow for more. Tone: [adjectives]. First person.' Review and lightly adjust.
2
Run the repurposing prompt
Paste your pillar article, then: 'Repurpose the above into: (1) 3 Instagram captions (different angles, max 120 words, question at end), (2) a 60-sec Reel script, (3) an email subject line + 80-word intro, (4) 2 Twitter posts with the most quotable lines.'
3
Generate your visuals in Canva AI
Open Canva (free). Start from your brand kit. Use AI text suggestion to create one branded post graphic. Use the same design as a template for all 3 Instagram posts — swap the text only.
4
Review and lightly edit
Read everything out loud. Change anything that does not sound like you. This takes 5 to 8 minutes and dramatically improves quality. Do not skip it.
✓ Step Deliverable
A full week of content: 3 Instagram captions, 1 LinkedIn post, 1 Reel script, 1 email intro, 2 Twitter posts — ready to schedule.
Step 03 · 15 minutes
Schedule and Automate
⏱ 15 minutes

Your content is written. Now you make it go out automatically — with zero manual posting for the rest of the week.

1
Sign up for Buffer (free)
Go to buffer.com and connect your Instagram and LinkedIn accounts. The free plan supports 3 channels and unlimited scheduled posts.
2
Set your posting schedule
In Buffer, go to Settings > Posting Schedule. Set: Instagram — Monday, Wednesday, Friday at 8am and 6pm. LinkedIn — Tuesday and Thursday at 8am. Adjust for your audience timezone.
3
Load this week's content
In Buffer's Compose window: paste caption, upload graphic, add 5 to 8 targeted hashtags, set the scheduled date and time. Hit Schedule. Repeat for each post. Your week is now automated.
4
Set a 15-minute daily engagement block
Each day in the first hour after a post goes live, reply to every comment using this prompt: 'Write a warm reply to this comment on my [topic] post. Tone: [adjectives]. Under 30 words. Conversational.' Engagement in the first hour determines reach.
✓ Step Deliverable
30 days of content scheduled and posting automatically. 60 minutes every Monday is all it takes from here.
